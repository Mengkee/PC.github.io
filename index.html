Mortgage question

For the interest-only mortagage, each payment repays all the interest accumulated, and the capital is paid out at the end of the term.

For others, each mortgage payment is a combination of interest and debt repayment, calculated so that the mortgage will be fully repaid at the end of the term.

The common mistake is assuming entire mortgage payment consists of interest.

To illustrate your point, you need to calculate the exact composition of the interest and the mortgage repayment in the each monthly payment.

As the outstanding debt reduces, the interest component falls and the debt repayment increases by the same amount.

Higher the interest, lower the initial debt repayment.

Pension question

The purpose of pension is to provide secure income to the investors when they retire.

More interest is earned when the pension pot is relatively large.

Investment returns at later durations will have a bigger impact on the value of the fund.

Do not give suggestions without the information on the current maturity yield, your clients' risk appetite etc.

When analysing the question, pay attention to your client's health status.

Bond market question

Two factors affecting the price of the government bond.

The price of the bond varies on daily basis, corresponding to the changes in the interest rate.

Higher the term to maturity, higher volatility inherent in the bond price.

The bond price converges to its par value, as the term to maturity decreases to zero.

Instead of yield (IRR), you should try to form your answer in terms of cash return. The cash returns include both interest and the capital gain/loss because this is what the investor cares about

The fears for uncertain inflation can also influence the price of bond, but it is a less significant factor.

For the corporate bond, the risk of default comes into play.

Life insurance question

For both the whole-life and endowment policies, the premiums build up a reserve which grows over the life of the policy. This reserve includes interest earned on the premiums, but also has various charges and expenses deducted from it.

Surrender values and paid-up policy values are based on the policy reserve at the time of surrender or lapse.

The net premium reserve is the accumulated net premiums less the accumulated cost of life cover.

A surrender value is a discretionary payment that the insurer will certainly reduce if the reserve is lower than anticipated. A maturity value, by contrast, must always be paid in full, even if the insurer has to take a loss.

The surrender value for the endowmment policy / whole life policy will increase over time.

Surrender values are not normally paid on term life or life annuity policies. The reserve for the term policy will increase until reaching a peak, and decrease.

Cost of life cover: charges were deducted from the whole-life reserve to cover the cost of providing life insurance

Moratality income for annuities: qx+t.Rx+t+1 is the mortality income between t and t+1, which enables annuities to pay a higher income than the investment return on the assets in the annuity fund (mainly bonds). The mortality income arises because the reserves held for annuitants who die are retained by the insurance company.

Mortality drag: An individual may prefer to draw income from a pension pot rather than buy a life annuity. Can the individual draw the same income that a life annuity would have provided without later being worse off? Only if the investment return on the pension pot is greater than the investment return on the life annuity reserves. The extra investment return required on the pension pot is called mortality drag.

Sensitivity to assumptions

Actuaries should be able to explain the sensitivity of their calculations to changes in different assumptions.

Sensitivity to interest rate

The sensitivity of the price (P) of an asset to a change in the force of interest (δ) is measured by its volatility. Volatility ≈ percentage increase in asset price caused by reducing discount rate by one percentage point. Volatility = Discounted Mean Term

DMT increases with N and falls with δ.

Sensitivity to mortality.

The effect of lighter mortality on pricing life assurance contracts depends on factors such as:

  • the type of policy
  • the initial age of the policyholder
  • the term of the policy

Sensitivity to expenses.

  • Providers of financial products incur expenses which are recouped from the customer in one way or another
  • Initial expenses arise from activities such as administration, underwriting and the payment of fees/commission to advisers/ agents. For most contracts, they are the bulk of the expenses.
  • Expenses can be recouped through transparent charges, loadings to premiums or reductions in benefits.
  • Consumer loans
  • The interest rate charged in consumer loans would normally be set at a level to cover expenses.
  • However, there may also be transparent fees, such as:
    - an initial arrangement fee for a mortgage, which may be added to the customer’s debt;
    - an annual credit card fee, possibly offset by an annual cashback payment on transactions.
  • Savings products
  • The interest rate paid on cash accounts is normally net of expenses.
  • Some managed funds sell units at a higher price than they redeem them (the “bid/offer spread”) to cover initial expenses.
  • In addition, the fund manager takes an annual charge equal to a fixed percentage of the assets in the fund.
  • The bid price at which units are redeemed is net of all charges.
  • Life assurance policies
  • Premiums for life assurance policies have loadings to cover initial and renewal expenses.
  • Initial expenses include underwriting and administration costs.
  • In the UK, commission is only paid on non-profits policies. Both initial and renewal commissions are paid.
  • No commissions are paid on with-profits and unit-linked policies, but the customer’s financial adviser may charge a transparent fee.
  • Underwriting costs depend on the age of applicant, size of policy and information given in the application form.
  • The lowest underwriting costs occur when the insurer believes that the information in the application form is sufficient.
  • The highest underwriting costs occur when the insurer requires the applicant to have a medical examination.
  • Life annuities
  • There are no underwriting costs for standard annuities sold to lives in good health.
  • Underwriting is only required for enhanced annuities sold to impaired lives subject to higher mortality.
  • Underwriting costs, administration costs and commission are deducted from the sum invested.
  • Hence the income paid to the policyholder is net of all expenses.
